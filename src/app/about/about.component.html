About

The Institute for Aging Research (IFAR) employs common user credentials accross all publically-accessible Web applications. These applications include electronic data capture, document sharing and reporting. This security configuration allows IT and research teams to control and monitor user authentication accross multiple services and platforms. Furthermore, this schema eliminates the need for separate username and passwords for individual projects, and it allows non-HSL employess to obtain appropriate access to our collaborative tools without comprimising coorporate intranet and mission critical systems.

IFAR research teams have autonomy and are permitted to authorize individuals access to applications or projects within the extranet. However, all application users must first sign up for an IFAR extranet account. The @Html.ActionLink("sign up", "register","account") process is easy and accounts are approved immediately. Users are identified by a unique email address and username. Users are categorized automatically as HSL employess or affiliates of the organization.

Any public user may sign up for an extranet account. IFAR does not restrict account affiliate. However, new individual accounts are not automatically allowed access to specific applications, services or projects therein. Application or project administrators must explicity grant new users access. These adminstrators are responsible for maintaining rights and following corporate security policy.

HSL IT and IFAR Research Administration may revoke or disable extranet accounts at any time. A disabled account will be unable to access all services. The extranet system generates automatic email messages and audit trails when accounts are added, updated, disabled or removed.

Please visit the contact page for additional information regarding technical support and policies.
